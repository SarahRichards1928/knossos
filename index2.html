Knossos is a scientific application for the rapid reconstruction of neural morphology and connectivity.

Knossos can be used to reconstruct cell morphologies from 3D electron microscopic data that has been generated by Serial Block-Face Electron Microscopy (SBEM).

By dynamically loading only data from the surrounding of the current view point, visualization is not limited to datasets that fit into the available RAM

In addition to viewing and navigating, Knossos allows efficient manual neurite annotation (“skeletonization”).

Knossos is being developed at the Max-Planck Institute for Medical Research in Heidelberg, Germany, and used by several groups for the annotation of SBEM datasets.
